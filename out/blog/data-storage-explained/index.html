Data Storage Explained

Understanding bytes, kilobytes, megabytes, gigabytes, and beyond

What is a Byte?

A byte is the basic unit of digital information. It consists of 8 bits (binary digits), and can represent 256 different values (0-255). Everything on your computer, from text documents to high-resolution videos, is ultimately stored as a series of bytes.

Understanding data storage units helps you manage your device storage, estimate download times, and compare storage options when buying new devices.

Binary vs Decimal: Why 1,024 instead of 1,000?

Computers work in binary (base-2), so storage units are based on powers of 2. This is why we use 1,024 (2^10) instead of 1,000 when converting between units.

The Math

  • • 1 KB = 2^10 bytes = 1,024 bytes
  • • 1 MB = 2^20 bytes = 1,048,576 bytes
  • • 1 GB = 2^30 bytes = 1,073,741,824 bytes

However, storage manufacturers often use decimal (base-10) for marketing, which is why a "500 GB" drive shows as ~465 GB on your computer.
